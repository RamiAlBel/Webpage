About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), Section for Visual Computing, based in Copenhagen. My work lies at the intersection of geometric deep learning, explainable AI, and high‑performance computing, with applications in astrophysics and medical imaging.

  • Education: PhD in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science (DTU); MSc in Astrophysics (Niels Bohr Institute, University of Copenhagen); BA in Physics (Aristotle University of Thessaloniki) with Erasmus studies at Eberhard Karls Universität Tübingen.
  • Research: geometric deep learning and explainability for 2D/3D data, parameter estimation for black holes from hotspot kinematics, and large‑scale simulations of star formation and planetary atmospheres.
  • Tools & interests: Python and C++ for scientific computing, hydrodynamic and radiative‑transfer codes (PLUTO, RAMSES, RADMC‑3D), and outside of research, composing music, chess, and table tennis.

You can also find me on DTU Orbit and Google Scholar.
