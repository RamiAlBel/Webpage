Movies

Short clips illustrating some of my research and numerical experiments.

Photoevaporation of planetary atmospheres

A hot star strips the atmosphere from a close-in planet through intense radiation. This movie shows how the gas is heated, accelerated, and ultimately blown away into space.

Supernova-driven galactic winds

Repeated supernova explosions in a galaxy drive powerful galactic winds. Here we track how hot gas, density, and outflow speed evolve as explosions carve channels through the interstellar medium.

Seeing through an interstellar cloud

An interstellar cloud can hide distant stars at visible wavelengths but becomes transparent in the infrared. This animation sweeps through wavelength to show when the stars reappear.

Mandelbrot fractal zoom beyond numerical precision

A deep zoom into the Mandelbrot set, revealing ever more intricate fractal structure until numerical precision finally breaks down.
